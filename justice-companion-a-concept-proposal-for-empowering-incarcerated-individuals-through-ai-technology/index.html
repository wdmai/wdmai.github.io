Justice Companion: A Concept Proposal for Empowering Incarcerated Individuals through AI Technology

Dr. Wending Mai
  1. Context / Background

Access to timely and relevant legal and emotional support remains a significant gap for incarcerated individuals, particularly those from marginalized or immigrant communities. Many prisons operate under resource-constrained environments, limiting inmates' ability to receive answers to critical legal, administrative, and personal questions. With increasing interest in rehabilitative justice and digital transformation within correctional systems, there is a unique opportunity to leverage emerging technologies to bridge this information gap.

Justice Companion (JC) is an AI-powered messaging tool designed to support inmates through their family or friends who communicate with them via approved prison communication platforms such as SmartInmate. JC enhances the quality and usefulness of those communications by enabling an AI-powered relay that receives inmate messages, processes them, and returns informative and empathetic responses—automatically and with the family member's consent.

  1. Challenges Faced by Inmates
  • Legal Isolation: Many incarcerated individuals lack access to legal representation or timely legal information.
  • Language and Literacy Barriers: Non-native English speakers and individuals with low literacy often understand legal documents or administrative procedures better via reading text message than listening.
  • Mental and Emotional Strain: Incarceration takes a heavy toll on mental health, especially when inmates feel forgotten or misunderstood.
  • Administrative Complexity: Navigating prison rules, filing grievances, or managing reentry processes can be overwhelming.
  • Disconnection from Community: Many inmates struggle to stay meaningfully connected with family and support networks.
  1. Change Solution: Leveraging AI

Justice Companion introduces an AI chatbot designed to support families and friends of inmates by automating the communication process. With the explicit consent of the family member during the initial setup, JC intercepts inmate messages received through approved platforms, routes them to the AI system for contextualized responses, and then transmits the AI-generated reply back on behalf of the family member. This process:

  • Reduces the burden on families to craft helpful, informed responses
  • Provide emotional support
  • Answer frequently asked questions about prison life or legal status
  • Communicate to support religious or psychological needs
  • Leverages public, non-legal databases and resources to guide responses
  • Direct users to verified legal aid organizations
  • Offer simple explanations of rights, procedures, and reentry planning
  • Encourages consistent communication

The AI functions only as a companion tool to aid communication—not as a substitute for legal or psychological advice.

  1. Approach and Process Elements
  • Initial Consent: Family members agree to automated message processing during first-time setup via terms of use and disclaimers.
  • Automated Routing: Inmate messages received by the family member’s account are automatically routed to the AI engine.
  • AI Message Processing: Responses are generated using structured prompts and vetted data sources.
  • Reply Relay: The AI-generated message is sent back through the user’s account to the inmate.
  • Transparency & Logs: Family members receive regular updates summarizing interactions.
  •  
  1. Implementation Plan
  • Phase 1 – Pilot: Recruit a small cohort of families in collaboration with a nonprofit partner. Collect qualitative feedback.
  • Phase 2 – Technical Integration: Refine the tool, improve AI prompt libraries, and build a knowledge base.
  • Phase 3 – Outreach & Partnerships: Engage faith-based prison ministries and legal aid organizations for scale.
  • Phase 4 – Education & Support: Offer onboarding resources, consent guidelines, and safety training.
  •  
  1. Risk Management
  • Legal Clarity: Messages include disclaimers clarifying that JC does not provide legal advice.
  • Informed Consent: All automation is opt-in with recorded agreement to terms.
  • Privacy Protections: No inmate data is stored; message content is user-generated and encrypted.
  • Platform Compliance: Designed to adapt various prison platforms.
  • Monitoring: Responses are reviewed periodically to ensure accuracy and emotional safety.
  1. Feedback and Impact Monitoring
  • User Surveys: Collect feedback from family members and track emotional, informational, and relational impact.
  • Chat Review Logs: Anonymized analysis of message themes to understand usage trends.
  • Ministry Reports: Partner with chaplains or nonprofits for qualitative case studies.
  • Usage Metrics: Track frequency of use, message satisfaction, and follow-up outcomes.
  1. Reporting and Accountability
  • Quarterly Impact Reports: Share anonymized data with stakeholders.
  • Ethics and Oversight Board: Include representatives from criminal justice reform, AI ethics, and pastoral care.
  • Open Feedback Channel: Users can submit suggestions or complaints directly.

Justice Companion offers a low-risk, high-impact solution to the problem of communication isolation in prisons. Through a careful combination of empathy, transparency, and ethical AI deployment, we can build a scalable tool that enhances human connection and supports inmate dignity.
